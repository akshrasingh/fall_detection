MobileNetV2

MobileNetV2 is a convolutional neural network architecture designed for efficient and lightweight deep learning tasks, particularly suited for mobile and embedded applications.

It is an evolution of the original MobileNet architecture, developed by researchers at Google. MobileNetV2 improves upon its predecessor by introducing several key enhancements aimed at improving performance and efficiency.

Resnet

ResNet, short for Residual Networks, is a deep neural network architecture known for its breakthrough use of residual connections.
These connections allow for the training of very deep networks (up to hundreds of layers) by mitigating the vanishing gradient problem.
By introducing skip connections that bypass certain layers, ResNet facilitates smoother gradient flow, enabling effective training of extremely deep neural networks.

EfficientNetB0

EfficientNetB0 is a convolutional neural network architecture renowned for its exceptional performance-efficiency trade-off.
With a systematic scaling of network depth, width, and resolution, EfficientNetB0 achieves superior accuracy while maintaining computational efficiency.
This scalability enables EfficientNetB0 to adapt to diverse resource constraints, making it suitable for various applications, including mobile and edge devices.
